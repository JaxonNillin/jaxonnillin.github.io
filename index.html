The Rise of a Failed Man. pt. 1

The End of my Third Decade.

Posted: 13 / February / 2026

Another year in the books with little to nothing to show for it, infact in some ways I have even less than I did a year ago. (on a positive, that includes significantly less fat, having lost 30kg and having gained some muscle)

The only things I can confidently say are stronger now are my beliefs and my resolve to chase a future worth living for.

But by most of societies metrics I have nothing, No Wife, No Kids, No Job, No Car, No House.. I have a handfull of friends, and my health... though both of those feel more fragile than I am comfortable with.

I DO have Goals though, not quite a stand in for the big dreams that seem to drive other people my age, like "starting a family" or "buying a house". But they're enough to give me direction for as long as I can keep choosing to forge ahead.

Currently I'm working on getting a better body composition through regular weight-lifting and I'm also learning to Code.

Here's hoping that leads to more options in the romance and employment areas of life.

Also trying to get more practice driving so I can get a restricted license and eventually a car of my own, the increase in freedom and self-reliance that I imagine comes with reliable transport opens entire new worlds for me.

Although the WONDERS that cycling all my life has done for my Hamstrings is much appreciated..

Goals Clarified

  • A Body I'm Proud to Inhabit
  • Finishing the beginner sections of Khan Academy's Computer Science / Programming Course
  • A Restricted License
  • A Job or a Larger Income
  • Increase my Savings

The Rise of a Failed Man. pt. 2

The Beginning of my Fourth Decade

Posted: 14/ February / 2026

The Day started with some aches and pains and a lot of uncertainty.

Will I be able to keep weight off if I keep losing weight or should I just give up and indulge my cravings for Cheese, Energy Drinks and Pies?

I know these questions and a handful of others will be on my mind for quite awhile, but for today I'm going to let myself go a bit. I have a lunch planned with my Mother and Sister, so I'll enjoy it to it's fullest instead of curtailing my appetite.

Hopefully next year I'll be dining with a woman who isn't family.
